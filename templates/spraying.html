Types of Pesticides

Herbicides

Herbicides stop the growth of weeds that can rob crops of the nutrients they need to grow. Furthermore, among the chemicals used as herbicides in agriculture are 2,4-D, Clethodim, Glyphosate, Bentazon, and Clethodim.


Insecticides

Insecticides, as the name implies, target the nervous system of insects to prevent them from inhibiting or reproducing. Among the chemicals used as insecticides include organophosphate, carbamates, nicotinoids, fumigants, and biological.


Fungicides

Fungicides aid in totally eliminating fungi by preventing their growth. Additionally, they quickly decompose in soil with air present. A few forms of insecticides that are also employed as fungicides include maneb, nabam, and cycloheximide.


Rodenticides

They are chemicals that are used to keep rodent populations under control, including fish, birds, and mammals that can damage crops as well as rats, mice, and gophers. Additionally, a few examples of rodenticides include chlorophacinone, diphacinone, brodifacoum, and bromadiolone.


Larvicides

Larvicides are pesticides that prevent vector-borne illnesses like malaria by limiting or controlling mosquito larvae or breeding.


Bactericides

Pesticides eradicate hazardous microorganisms from crops and livestock or prevent their spread.


Molluscicides

pesticides that typically prevent mollusks like snails, slugs, and others from living in an area or from spreading there.


Algaecides

For increased crop output, these herbicides destroy algae or prevent their growth. Now that we have a list of pesticides and their applications, let's explore the persistent and biodegradable pesticide categories.

Fertilizers

Fertilizers are natural or manmade compounds that are used to provide nutrients to plants. It is used to boost crop productivity in agricultural fields. Farmers that engage in agricultural techniques or farming utilise fertiliser on a daily basis to boost crop yield. Fertilizers are items that are required for the improvement of soil and the retention of water for plant growth. It gives plants nutrients like nitrogen, potassium, and phosphorus to help them grow.

Urea can be administered to the soil as solid granules, prills, or pellets. Urea fertilizer¬†contains nitrogen, which promotes root growth. It aids in the formation of plant leaves, stalks, and stems. Urea fertilizer¬†gives plants a dark green color by creating chlorophyll. DAP

The full name of DAP fertiliser is Diamonimum Phosphate, and it is a popular fertiliser among farmers. It has a great nutritional value and is often used by farmers. The DAP is a great phosphate and nitrogen source. DAP has an alkaline pH and quickly dissolves in soil, releasing phosphate and ammonium for the plants. The DAP should not be used too close to the roots since the ammonium release might harm the seedlings and plant roots, and damage occurs when the pH exceeds 7. DAP is a great source of nitrogen that will be progressively transformed into nitrate by soil bacteria, resulting in a brief rise in pH. With adequate management, the differences in DAP and Monoammonium Phosphate are negligible or there is no change in plant development and yield owing to Phosphorous source.
